Fairness Debugger Framework

How bAIsed Detects, Localizes, Simulates, and Repairs Bias.

bAIsed is not just a bias scorecard. Our backend combines deterministic fairness metrics, subgroup hotspot analysis, root-cause ranking, counterfactual simulation, and repair guidance so teams can debug why unfair outcomes happen and what change fixes them fastest.

calculate

Disparate Impact Ratio (DIR)

DIR remains the primary compliance-facing metric in the engine. We compute it from observed selection rates and use the 0.8 threshold to classify whether protected groups are falling behind the highest-performing reference group.

Equation
DIR = P(Y=1 | D=p) / P(Y=1 | D=u)

Threshold 0.8

According to the EEOC's "Four-Fifths Rule," a selection rate for any group which is less than 80% of the rate for the highest group will generally be regarded as evidence of adverse impact.

Multi-Metric Fairness Engine

The detection engine does not rely on DIR alone. We compute Equal Opportunity Difference and Average Odds Difference to detect fairness failures that only appear when qualified candidates or error tradeoffs are considered.

Root Cause Analysis

We rank non-protected features by disparity impact. For each candidate feature, the engine measures variance in outcome rates across its groups and scores how strongly it contributes to unfair outcomes.

experience_level impact 0.42
region impact 0.21
Most influential features are then used by the simulation engine to generate deterministic “what if we remove this influence?” scenarios.

Hidden Bias Rule

If global severity is LOW but any subgroup hotspot is HIGH, the engine raises hidden_bias_detected = true.

Counterfactual Goal

Each simulation recomputes fairness metrics after a deterministic intervention so teams can compare repair effectiveness before changing production policy.
